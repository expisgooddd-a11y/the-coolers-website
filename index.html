How Our Earthquake Project Works

Project Overview

Our project is based on: Tuned Mass Damper theory, Which uses a counterweight tuned to match the structure's sway, absorbing its motion. Newton’s Third Law, Seismic action forces generate equal reaction forces via inertial opposition. Seismic Energy Dissipation, Earthquake energy transforms into thermal energy through damping, preventing resonance damage.

Working Mechanism

This is how students will work with our project.:

  • The students will use a shake table to test different model buildings.
  • They can change how strong the earthquake is.
  • Students should watch closely which building wobbles, cracks, or falls over first.
  • They will write down what happens to each building during the demonstration.
  • At the end, we talk about why some buildings handles earthquakes better than others.

How We Feel About This Project

We Really Liked Doing This Project

Our whole team feels really great about finishing this project, and we all had a good time working on it from the start. The process was fun because we got to learn new things and try out our ideas, and it was cool to see something we talked about become real. We enjoyed figuring stuff out together and helping each other when someone was stuck, which made the whole thing more enjoyable and less like just regular school work.

Even though some parts were confusing or didn't work at first, we didn't get too stressed because we were doing it as friends. The best part was probably just hanging out and working together, talking about what to do next and celebrating the small wins. We’re all pretty proud of what we ended up with, and it feels good to have made something ourselves.

We Got Through the Hard Parts Together

It definitely wasn’t easy the whole time, we ran into alot of problems that made us have to think really hard. Sometimes the code wouldn’t work, or a piece of equipment broke, or we just couldn’t agree on what to do next. There were days where it felt like we weren’t getting anywhere and it was kinda frustrating.

But whenever things got tough, we made sure to concentrate and focus on fixing it as a group. Instead of getting mad or giving up, we talked it out and tried different ideas until something worked. Working together like that made the hard stuff easier to deal with, and in the end, it made finishing the project feel even better because we know we earned it. We learned that if you stick together and keep trying, you can figure out almost anything.

Challenges & Struggles We Faced

We Couldn't Find the Stuff We Needed

First big problem was getting the parts. Alot of times the things we needed to buy was sold out or cost to much money. This made it hard to start building. We needed to figure out what to do instead and change our plans.

Our Wiring Kept Messing Up

The electrical stuff was really tricky. Wires wouldn't connect good and sometimes things just stopped working for no reason. It took us a long time to find where the problem was at and fix it. We had to check everything one by one.

It Was Hard Getting Places and Moving Things

Getting everyone together was tough because we all live in different places. Also moving our project stuff from someone's house to the school was a hassle. We was often late or forgot to bring important pieces.

How We Fixed Everything

We got past all of this by working as a team and making good plans. For the missing parts, we made a super detailed list of what we actually needed. For the wiring, we all looked at it together until we seen the mistake. And for the transportation, we made a schedule so we all knew who was bringing what and when to meet. By managing our time good and helping eachother, we finally got our project to work.
